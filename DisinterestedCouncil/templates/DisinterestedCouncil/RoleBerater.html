Your role
A random procedure has predetermined whether each participant is part of a pair (Player X and Player Y) or an individual decision-maker (Player Z).
This random procedure assigned you to be
Player Y.
The assignment as Player X, Player Y, or Player Z will remain the same throughout the session.
In this experiment, there will be 10 decision making rounds. In each round, a pair consisting of Player X and Player Y will make decisions, which, together with the decision of the Player Z, determine each participantâ€™s payoff in a given round.
In each of the 10 decision making rounds, you and a Player Y- and a Player Z-participant will be randomly grouped. The identity of other participants in the group of three can change from round to round.
You will not learn of the identity of the other participants in your group. None of the other participants in your group will learn of your identity.
Your decision and others' decisions
You and Player X receive identical information about the payoffs associated with six different Projects (Projects A-F).
The six Projects A-F may differ regarding the payoffs they offer to Player X and to Player Z. Your payoff as Player Y is fixed at 70 E$ in each round, independent of which Project is implemented.
The decision which Project is implemented is made by Player Z.
The only information that Player Z will have for making the decision which Project to implement is the wording of a message that Player X sends to him or her.
The messages that Player X can send to Player Z read:
  • Message A: "Project A will earn you most."
  • Message B: "Project B will earn you most."
  • Message C: "Project C will earn you most."
  • Message D: "Project D will earn you most."
  • Message E: "Project E will earn you most."
  • Message F: "Project F will earn you most."
Your role: After seeing the information about Projects A-F, you make a recommendation to Player X which message he or she should send to Player Z.
After receiving your recommendation, Player X decides which message he/she sends to Player Z.
After receiving the message that Player X has sent, Player Z decides which of the six Projects is implemented.
